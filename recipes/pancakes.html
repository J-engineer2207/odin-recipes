Fluffy Pancakes

If you're looking for a quick and easy breakfast to fuel your day, this pancake recipe is perfect for college students. Pancakes are a classic choice—simple to make, customizable with your favorite toppings, and sure to give you the energy you need to tackle your studies or a busy schedule. With just a few basic ingredients and minimal equipment, you'll have a stack of delicious, fluffy pancakes ready in no time. Whether you're cooking for yourself or a group of friends, these pancakes are a budget-friendly, satisfying option that’s easy to prepare even in a small dorm kitchen.

This recipe is ideal for beginners or anyone looking for a stress-free breakfast that doesn't require a lot of time or complex steps. All you need is some flour, milk, sugar, and eggs, plus a touch of vanilla and butter to elevate the flavor. With these simple ingredients, you'll create pancakes that are soft, light, and golden brown. And the best part? You can experiment with different toppings like fruit, chocolate chips, or syrup to make them even more enjoyable. So grab your ingredients, fire up your stove, and get ready to enjoy a homemade breakfast that will leave you feeling satisfied and ready to take on the day!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, beat the egg and then add the milk, melted butter, and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients. Stir gently until just combined. Be careful not to over-mix; a few lumps are okay.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lightly grease with butter or oil.
  5. For each pancake, pour about 1/4 cup of batter onto the hot skillet. Cook for 2-3 minutes or until bubbles form on the surface. Flip the pancake and cook for another 1-2 minutes, until golden brown and cooked through.
  6. Serve the pancakes warm with your favourite toppings like syrup, fresh fruit, or butter.
